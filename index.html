What this project is

Clarity, calm and context for ED – not a sales funnel.

Most online ED content either minimises the problem or tries to sell something quickly. ED After 30 is different by design.

What you’ll get

Plain-English explanations of how erections work, why problems appear in your 30s–50s, and what your clinician is actually thinking about when they investigate ED.

What you won’t get

No instant promises, miracle supplements or pressure to buy medication. No hidden pharmacy deals, no upsell sequence in the background.

Why it exists

When men understand the link between ED, heart health, hormones, mental health and lifestyle, they stop blaming themselves and start making informed decisions instead of panic purchases.

Looking ahead

Education now. Thoughtful support later, if it genuinely helps.

Right now, ED After 30 is deliberately an educational project only. There is no paid programme or consultation pathway to sign up for – yet. That gives space to build resources that are actually useful first.

Over time, there may be options such as structured self-assessment, group education or carefully designed 1:1 support in collaboration with clinicians. If and when that happens, early subscribers will be the first to hear – with no obligation to take part.

  • Clear boundary: this site is not a clinic or emergency service.
  • Any future support model will prioritise safety, realism and honest expectations.
  • You are always free to use the education here simply to get more from your existing GP or specialist appointments.
Questions

Frequently asked questions

A few key clarifications about what ED After 30 is – and what it is not.

Is this a medical service or clinic?
No. ED After 30 is currently an educational project. It does not diagnose, treat or prescribe. The aim is to help you understand ED, reflect on your own situation and prepare for better conversations with your own clinician.
Will I get ED tablets or treatment here?
No. This site explains how treatments are usually chosen and what clinicians think about when prescribing, but it does not provide medication or devices.
Why talk about “future support” if there is nothing to book yet?
Because the long-term vision is to explore structured support options. Being transparent now sets realistic expectations and avoids surprise sales later. You are under no pressure to join anything that might develop.
How often will you email me?
Only when there is something useful to share: new guides, tools or invitations to small pilot projects. There is no daily sequence, and you can unsubscribe at any time from any email.
