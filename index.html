How this project works

Learn, reflect, then plan your next move.

ED After 30 is being built in public. Content and tools are shaped by men who join the early-access list and share what they actually need.

Step 1

Start with solid knowledge

Short, focused guides explain what ED is, why it happens and when it can signal something important about your overall health.

Step 2

Reflect on your own picture

Use checklists and prompts (coming soon) to map symptoms, lifestyle and medical history – so you see the whole picture, not just “performance”.

Step 3

Go to your clinician prepared

Bring clear notes to your GP or specialist, ask better questions and understand the reasoning behind investigations and treatments.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

A few important clarifications before you sign up or use the content.

Is this a medical service or clinic?
No. ED After 30 is currently an educational project. It does not diagnose, treat or prescribe. The aim is to help you understand ED and prepare for better conversations with your own clinician.
Will I get ED tablets or treatment here?
No. We do not supply medication or devices. We explain how treatments work, who they are usually for, and which safety issues your prescriber will consider.
What does “limited early-access spots” actually mean?
As new tools or pilot projects are developed, a small number of subscribers may be invited to test them and provide feedback. This is to keep the workload manageable and ensure thoughtful improvements, not to create artificial urgency.
How often will you email me?
Only when there is something useful to share – new guides, tools or invitations to early pilots. You can unsubscribe at any time from any email.
